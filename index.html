History of the Site

Environment Reports was established in early 2024 as a response to growing concerns over the lack of accessible, research-backed environmental information available to the public. What began as a small community blog has since evolved into a trusted platform used by educators, environmentalists, and students worldwide.

The site was born out of the realization that many environmental reports and climate studies remain locked behind academic paywalls. John Mullen, with support from environmental networks across Europe and Asia, launched this initiative to break those barriers and promote open-access sustainability reporting for all.

About the Site

Environment Reports is a not-for-profit, digital knowledge hub that focuses on environmental research, sustainability strategies, and climate change education. It offers curated publications, open-access documents, and expert articles from global contributors.

Our mission is to bridge the gap between science and society by making credible environmental research understandable and actionable. Whether you're a high school student working on a project or a policymaker drafting your next climate resolution, this site aims to empower you with the information you need.

About the Creator

John Mullen is a climate science communicator, independent researcher, and passionate advocate for ecological literacy. Based in Berlin, Germany, John has spent over 15 years working with NGOs, European think tanks, and grassroots climate movements.

He holds a Master's degree in Environmental Systems and Policy from the University of Freiburg and has contributed to sustainability frameworks used in EU urban planning initiatives. His work emphasizes data transparency, public education, and global collaboration on pressing environmental issues.

Outside of research, John actively mentors students and organizes annual climate education bootcamps across Europe and South Asia.
